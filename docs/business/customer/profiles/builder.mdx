# The Web3 Builder

## Description

Example: *Growth-Focused Startup Founder*, *Regional Retail Distributor*

**Category**

- Customer
- Strategic Partner
- Channel Partner
- Investor
- Influencer

**Industry**

Which industries they operate in.

**Company Size (if B2B)**

- Solo / Freelancer
- Small (1–10 employees)
- Medium (10–200 employees)
- Large (200+ employees)

**Location**

- Country / Region
- Urban / Suburban / Global

---

## Demographics

### For Individual Customers

- Age Range
- Education Level
- Job Title / Role
- Income Range

---

## 3. Goals & Motivations

What they are trying to achieve.

Examples:

- Grow revenue
- Save time
- Reduce costs
- Improve efficiency
- Expand into new markets
- Build brand authority

**Key Question**

> What does success look like for this person or organization?

---

## 4. Problems & Pain Points

What challenges they are actively trying to solve.

Examples:

- Lack of time
- Difficulty finding customers
- Inefficient processes
- Lack of technical expertise
- High operating costs
- Strong competition

Focus on **urgent or costly problems**.

---

## 5. Needs & Desired Outcomes

What they want from a solution or partnership.

Examples:

- Easy-to-use tools
- Reliable partners
- Faster growth
- Automation
- Better analytics
- Market expansion opportunities

---

## Decision Behavior

How they evaluate solutions.

### Decision Factors

- Price
- Quality
- Brand reputation
- ROI
- Ease of implementation
- Customer support

### Decision Process

- Research online
- Ask peers or industry contacts
- Compare competitors
- Test demos or free trials
- Internal approvals

---

## Channels

Where they discover solutions or spend time.

Examples:

- LinkedIn
- Industry events
- Podcasts
- YouTube
- Email newsletters
- Online communities
- Conferences
- Industry publications

---

## 8. What Makes Them a Great Fit

Signals that indicate someone is an **ideal target**.

Examples:

- Already spending money on similar solutions
- Rapidly growing company
- Actively hiring
- Looking for partnerships
- Has an existing distribution channel or audience

---

## 9. Red Flags (Bad Fit)

Who you should **not prioritize** targeting.

Examples:

- No available budget
- Too early-stage to benefit
- Not decision makers
- Requires heavy customization
- Misaligned goals or values

---

## 10. Messaging That Resonates

What type of messaging is most effective.

Examples:

- "Save time and automate your workflow"
- "Increase revenue without increasing headcount"
- "Scale faster with fewer resources"
- "Gain insights with powerful analytics"
- "Expand your reach through strategic partnerships"
